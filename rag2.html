Source Data

Chunking is the process of breaking large documents into smaller pieces called chunks. Chunks should contain enough information to preserve meaning but be small enough for efficient retrieval. Chunk size depends on the type of content. Books may be split by chapters, articles by paragraphs, and code by functions.
